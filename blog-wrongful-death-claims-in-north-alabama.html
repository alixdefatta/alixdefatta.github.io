Wrongful Death Claims in North Alabama: What Families Need to Know

Losing a loved one is always devastating—but when that loss is caused by someone else’s negligence or misconduct, the grief can be compounded by questions, confusion, and even anger. Understanding Wrongful Death in Alabama

Under Alabama law, a wrongful death occurs when a person dies due to the wrongful act, omission, or negligence of another. These cases can stem from car accidents, workplace injuries, medical malpractice, or defective products. Unlike some other states, Alabama’s wrongful death laws are unique in that they focus on punishing the wrongdoer, rather than compensating survivors for their personal losses. This means that damages in a wrongful death lawsuit are punitive in nature and meant to deter future misconduct.

Who Can File a Wrongful Death Claim?

In Alabama, a wrongful death claim must be filed by the personal representative of the deceased’s estate. This is often a close family member, such as a spouse, parent, or adult child. If you’re unsure who this is or how to start, DeFatta Law Firm can help you determine the best next steps.

Don’t Wait to Seek Legal Guidance

Time is a critical factor in wrongful death cases. Alabama has a two-year statute of limitations from the date of death to file a claim. Waiting too long could mean losing your right to seek justice.
