Alabama Dog Bite Law

Huntsville Dog Bite & Animal Attack Attorney

A dog bite can happen in an instant — but the physical and emotional aftermath can last a lifetime. Puncture wounds, lacerations, nerve damage, permanent scarring, and post-traumatic stress are all common consequences of a serious dog attack. Children are especially vulnerable, often suffering disfiguring facial injuries that require multiple surgeries.

Under Alabama Code Section 3-6-1, dog owners in Alabama are strictly liable when their dog bites or attacks a person who is lawfully on the property and has not provoked the animal. You do not need to prove the owner knew the dog was dangerous. The attack itself is enough to establish liability.

Common Dog Bite Injuries

Types of Injuries DeFatta Law Firm Handles

Dog attacks can cause a wide range of injuries — from minor punctures to life-altering disfigurement. No matter the severity, you may be entitled to significant compensation under Alabama law.

🩹

Puncture Wounds & Lacerations

Deep bites that penetrate skin, muscle, and tissue — often requiring stitches, surgery, and prolonged wound care to prevent infection and minimize scarring.

🦴

Broken Bones & Crush Injuries

Large dogs can fracture bones — especially in hands, wrists, and arms — when victims try to protect themselves during an attack.

😢

Facial Injuries & Scarring

Facial dog bites are especially common in children and can require multiple reconstructive surgeries. Permanent scarring significantly increases the value of your claim.

🧠

Nerve Damage

Bites to hands, arms, or face can sever or damage nerves, causing permanent numbness, weakness, or loss of sensation.

😰

Psychological Trauma & PTSD

Dog attack survivors — especially children — often develop lasting fear of dogs, anxiety, and post-traumatic stress disorder. These non-physical injuries are fully compensable.

🦠

Infections & Rabies Exposure

Dog bites carry serious infection risk including Pasteurella, Capnocytophaga, and rabies. Treatment can be extensive, painful, and costly.

Common Questions

Dog Bite Attorney FAQs

Here are answers to the most common questions dog bite victims ask DeFatta Law Firm about their rights under Alabama law.

Is the dog owner responsible if their dog bites me in Alabama? +
Yes. Under Alabama Code Section 3-6-1, dog owners are strictly liable when their dog bites or attacks a person who is lawfully on the property and did not provoke the dog. You do not need to prove the owner knew the dog was dangerous — the bite itself establishes liability. This applies throughout Huntsville, Athens, Decatur, Madison, Scottsboro, Harvest, and all of North Alabama.
What compensation can I recover after a dog bite in Alabama? +
You may be entitled to compensation for medical expenses, surgery, wound care, lost wages, permanent scarring or disfigurement, pain and suffering, and emotional distress including PTSD. DeFatta Law Firm will evaluate all damages and fight for the maximum recovery available in your case.
How long do I have to file a dog bite claim in Alabama? +
Alabama's statute of limitations for dog bite personal injury claims is two years from the date of the attack. Acting quickly is important — evidence disappears, witnesses forget, and animal control reports may be purged. Contact DeFatta Law Firm as soon as possible to protect your rights.
What is the average settlement for a dog bite in Huntsville, Alabama? +
Dog bite settlements vary widely. Minor bites with minimal scarring may settle in the range of a few thousand dollars. Severe attacks causing disfigurement, nerve damage, or psychological trauma can result in settlements of $50,000 to $150,000 or more. Every case is different — call DeFatta Law Firm for a free evaluation of your specific situation.
What if the dog owner doesn't have insurance? +
Most dog bite claims are paid through the dog owner's homeowner's or renter's insurance. If the owner has no insurance, you may still recover directly from them. DeFatta Law Firm will investigate all sources of potential compensation and advise you on the best legal strategy for your case.
How much does it cost to hire a dog bite attorney? +
DeFatta Law Firm handles dog bite cases on a contingency-fee basis — you pay nothing upfront and owe no attorney fees unless we win your case. Your initial consultation is completely free. We believe every injured person deserves access to quality legal representation regardless of their financial situation.
