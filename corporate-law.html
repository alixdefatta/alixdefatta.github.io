When Disputes Arise

Business Conflicts We Handle

Business disputes can be costly, time-consuming, and damaging to your reputation. DeFatta Law Firm pursues efficient, decisive outcomes — whether through negotiation, arbitration, or the courtroom.

📋

Breach of Contract

When another party fails to uphold their obligations, we pursue full enforcement of your contractual rights — and seek damages for every cost their breach has caused.

🤝

Partnership & Shareholder Disputes

Disagreements between co-owners can paralyze a business. We help resolve governance conflicts, buyout disputes, and fiduciary duty breaches efficiently and decisively.

🔒

Trade Secret & Non-Compete Claims

Protecting your intellectual property and confidential business information is critical. We pursue and defend trade secret misappropriation and non-compete agreement violations.

🚫

Fraud & Misrepresentation

If another party made false representations that caused you financial harm — in a sale, a partnership, or a transaction — we pursue claims for fraud and financial recovery.

📉

Tortious Interference

When a competitor or third party wrongfully interferes with your business relationships or contracts, you have legal recourse. We hold bad actors accountable and seek compensation.

🏦

Business Acquisition Disputes

Deals that go wrong — misrepresented financials, undisclosed liabilities, failed earn-outs — require immediate legal action. We protect buyers and sellers in post-closing disputes.

👔

Employment Disputes

From wrongful termination and wage disputes to non-compete enforcement, we handle the employment-related litigation that can arise in any business environment.

⚠️

Unfair Competition

False advertising, unfair business practices, and deceptive trade tactics harm your business and your reputation. We pursue claims under Alabama and federal unfair competition law.

Common Questions

Corporate Law FAQs

Answers to the questions we hear most from North Alabama business owners and entrepreneurs.

What type of business entities can DeFatta Law Firm help me form in Alabama? +
DeFatta Law Firm can assist with forming all types of business entities in Alabama, including LLCs, corporations (S-corp and C-corp), partnerships, sole proprietorships, and professional associations. We'll analyze your specific situation — including liability exposure, tax implications, and long-term growth goals — to help you choose the structure that best protects your interests and sets your business up for success.
Why does my small business need a corporate attorney? +
Even small businesses face significant legal risks — from poorly drafted contracts to partner disputes to compliance failures. A corporate attorney helps you get it right from the start, reducing the risk of costly disputes down the road. DeFatta Law Firm works with businesses of all sizes across North Alabama, providing practical legal counsel tailored to your budget and goals. Think of us as your legal partner, not just an outside attorney.
What kinds of business disputes does DeFatta Law Firm handle? +
We handle a wide range of business disputes, including breach of contract claims, partnership and shareholder disputes, trade secret and non-compete litigation, fraudulent misrepresentation, tortious interference with business relationships, unfair competition claims, and disputes over business acquisitions or dissolutions. We pursue cost-effective resolutions whenever possible — but when litigation is necessary, we fight aggressively to protect your business interests.
How can DeFatta Law Firm help with contracts? +
We assist with all aspects of business contracting — drafting, reviewing, and negotiating agreements of all types. This includes vendor and supplier agreements, employment contracts, non-compete agreements, service agreements, partnership agreements, shareholder agreements, NDAs, licensing agreements, and real estate contracts. We'll identify problematic clauses, negotiate better terms, and help you avoid the common pitfalls that create liability or disputes later.
What should I know before buying or selling a business in Alabama? +
Buying or selling a business is one of the most significant transactions you'll ever undertake. Key legal considerations include due diligence (reviewing financials, contracts, liabilities, and legal claims), deal structure (asset purchase vs. stock purchase), representations and warranties, indemnification provisions, non-compete agreements, and regulatory compliance. DeFatta Law Firm guides you through every phase of the transaction to protect your investment and ensure a smooth closing.
Does DeFatta Law Firm offer ongoing legal counsel for businesses? +
Yes. Many of our business clients rely on DeFatta Law Firm for ongoing legal counsel — from reviewing contracts and advising on compliance matters to guiding major business decisions. Having a trusted attorney familiar with your business on call can prevent costly mistakes and give you the confidence to move forward decisively. Contact us to discuss how we can support your business on an ongoing basis.
