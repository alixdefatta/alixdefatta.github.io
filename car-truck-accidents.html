After an Accident

What to Do After a Car Accident
in North Alabama

A car accident can be overwhelming, and it can be hard to know what to do next. Following these steps protects your health, your legal rights, and your chances of fair compensation.

01

Ensure Safety

Move to a safe location away from traffic, check for injuries, and turn on your hazard lights. Your safety comes first — call 911 immediately if anyone is hurt.

02

Seek Medical Attention

Even if you feel fine, get evaluated. Injuries like whiplash or internal trauma may not show immediate symptoms. Prompt medical documentation is also critical for your claim.

03

Document the Scene

Take photos of vehicles, damages, road conditions, and visible injuries. Gather witness information and write down every detail you can remember while the scene is fresh.

04

Exchange Information

Collect names, contact numbers, insurance details, and vehicle information from all involved parties. Accurate information is key for determining liability and filing claims.

05

Report the Accident

Contact law enforcement to file an official police report. A police report provides an impartial account of the incident and is critical evidence in your case.

06

Consult an Attorney

Contact DeFatta Law Firm as soon as possible. Early legal guidance ensures you avoid costly mistakes and puts you in the strongest position to recover maximum compensation.

Common Crash Injuries

Injuries We Help Victims Recover From

Car and truck accidents can cause injuries that range from painful to permanently life-altering. DeFatta Law Firm helps victims pursue full compensation for all of them.

Whiplash & Soft Tissue Injuries

Whiplash is among the most common injuries in rear-end collisions. The rapid back-and-forth motion of the neck damages muscles, ligaments, and tendons — leading to pain, stiffness, and reduced mobility that can become chronic if left untreated.

Traumatic Brain Injuries (TBIs)

A blow or jolt to the head during a crash can cause TBIs ranging from mild concussions to permanent neurological damage. Symptoms include headaches, memory loss, confusion, and cognitive challenges — often requiring long-term medical care.

Spinal Cord & Back Injuries

Damage to the spinal cord can range from herniated discs to paralysis. These injuries are often life-altering, requiring ongoing treatment and potentially impacting your ability to work or live independently.

Broken Bones & Fractures

High-impact accidents frequently cause fractures that require surgery, physical therapy, and significant recovery time. Broken bones can limit mobility and lead to long-term complications when not properly treated and compensated.

What You May Be Owed

What Compensation Can You Claim After a Car Accident?

If you've been injured in a car or truck accident, understanding what you're entitled to is essential. At DeFatta Law Firm, we work tirelessly to pursue every category of compensation available under Alabama law.

Medical Expenses

Emergency care, surgeries, physical therapy, and ongoing treatments — both current costs and future medical needs related to your injuries.

Lost Wages & Earning Capacity

Compensation for income lost during recovery, and for long-term disability that impacts your ability to work at full capacity in the future.

Pain & Suffering

Monetary recognition of physical pain, emotional distress, anxiety, and the diminished quality of life resulting from your injuries.

Property Damage

Reimbursement for repairing or replacing your vehicle and any other personal property damaged in the crash.

Punitive Damages

In cases involving gross negligence or recklessness — such as drunk driving — punitive damages may be awarded to hold the at-fault party fully accountable.

Common Questions

Car & Truck Accident FAQs

We know this is a stressful time. Here are answers to the questions our clients ask most often about car and truck accident claims in Alabama.

Do I need a lawyer for a car accident in Huntsville, AL? +
Even if your accident seems minor, consulting with a car accident attorney is often a good idea. Injuries like whiplash or internal trauma may not show immediate symptoms but can lead to significant medical expenses. Insurance companies also frequently offer low settlements that don't cover your full losses. Having a lawyer ensures your rights are protected and helps you pursue fair compensation.
How long does it take to settle a car accident claim in Alabama? +
The timeline varies depending on the complexity of the case, the severity of injuries, and the insurance company's willingness to negotiate. Some claims settle in a few months, while others may take a year or more if litigation is necessary. DeFatta Law Firm works diligently to resolve your case as quickly as possible while ensuring you receive full and fair compensation.
What does it cost to hire a car accident attorney at DeFatta Law Firm? +
DeFatta Law Firm works on a contingency fee basis — meaning you pay no upfront fees. Our payment is a percentage of the settlement or verdict we secure for you. If we don't win your case, you owe us nothing. Your initial consultation is completely free and confidential.
Can I recover compensation if I was partially at fault for the accident? +
Alabama follows a contributory negligence rule, which can limit your ability to recover damages if you are found partially at fault. This makes it critical to consult a knowledgeable attorney as soon as possible. DeFatta Law Firm will investigate the facts, build a strong case, and advocate to protect your right to compensation.
What if the other driver doesn't have insurance? +
If the at-fault driver is uninsured or underinsured, you may still recover compensation through your own uninsured/underinsured motorist (UM/UIM) coverage. Our team will help you navigate this process and pursue every available avenue for compensation so you aren't left bearing the costs alone.
What is the deadline to file a car accident lawsuit in Alabama? +
In Alabama, the statute of limitations for personal injury claims from car accidents is generally two years from the date of the accident. For wrongful death claims, the deadline is also two years from the date of death. Missing this deadline can permanently bar your claim — contact DeFatta Law Firm as soon as possible after your accident.
