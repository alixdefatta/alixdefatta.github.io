Types of Elder Abuse

Understanding Nursing Home Abuse & Neglect

Abuse in nursing homes can be difficult to recognize, especially when a loved one is unable to communicate clearly. Understanding the different forms it can take is the first step toward protecting those who cannot protect themselves.

🤕

Physical Abuse

Intentional acts that cause bodily harm, including hitting, slapping, restraining, or the improper use of medications to subdue residents.

💔

Emotional & Psychological Abuse

Verbal threats, humiliation, intimidation, isolation, or any conduct that causes emotional distress or fear in a resident.

🚨

Sexual Abuse

Any non-consensual sexual contact or behavior directed at a nursing home resident, regardless of whether the resident has dementia or is otherwise incapacitated.

💸

Financial Exploitation

Unauthorized use of a resident's funds, property, or assets — including theft, forgery, fraud, or coercing a resident to change their will or financial accounts.

⚠️

Neglect & Abandonment

Failure to provide basic necessities including food, water, hygiene, medical care, and supervision — often resulting in bedsores, malnutrition, falls, or infection.

⚖️

Wrongful Death

When neglect or abuse causes the death of a nursing home resident, the family may pursue a wrongful death claim to hold the facility accountable.

Know the Red Flags

Warning Signs of Nursing Home Abuse

Abuse is often hidden behind closed doors. Victims may be afraid, confused, or unable to speak up. As a family member or loved one, you are often the first — and only — line of defense. Watch for these warning signs:

Unexplained bruises, cuts, burns, or broken bones
Sudden or dramatic weight loss, dehydration, or malnutrition
Bedsores or pressure ulcers from prolonged immobility
Poor hygiene, soiled clothing, or unsanitary living conditions
Fearfulness, withdrawal, or anxiety around specific staff members
Sudden changes in mood, personality, or behavior
Unexplained financial transactions or missing funds
Staff refusing to allow private visits or conversations

Common Questions

Nursing Home Abuse FAQs

We know this is an overwhelming time. Here are answers to the questions families most commonly ask about nursing home abuse and neglect claims in Alabama.

What are the signs of nursing home abuse or neglect? +
Warning signs include unexplained bruises, cuts, burns, or broken bones; sudden weight loss or dehydration; bedsores or pressure ulcers; poor hygiene or soiled clothing; sudden changes in behavior or mood; fearfulness around certain staff members; and unexplained financial transactions. If you notice any of these, contact DeFatta Law Firm immediately for a free consultation. Trust your instincts — if something feels wrong, it probably is.
Who can file a nursing home abuse claim in Alabama? +
In Alabama, the nursing home resident, their legal guardian, power of attorney, or next of kin can file a nursing home abuse claim. If the resident has passed away due to neglect or mistreatment, the executor of the estate may file a wrongful death claim on behalf of the family. DeFatta Law Firm can help you determine the right legal pathway for your specific situation.
How long do I have to file a nursing home abuse claim in Alabama? +
Alabama's statute of limitations for nursing home abuse and neglect claims is generally two years from the date of injury or discovery of abuse. For wrongful death cases, the deadline is two years from the date of death. Acting quickly is critical — evidence can disappear, and staff and records can change. Contact DeFatta Law Firm as soon as possible to protect your family's rights.
What compensation is available in a nursing home abuse case? +
Depending on the nature of the case, compensation may include medical expenses for treatment of injuries caused by abuse or neglect, pain and suffering, emotional distress, rehabilitation costs, and punitive damages to punish particularly egregious conduct. In wrongful death cases involving nursing home neglect, punitive damages are available under Alabama law. DeFatta Law Firm will thoroughly evaluate your case and pursue the maximum compensation available.
What if the nursing home asks me to sign something after an incident? +
Do not sign anything from the nursing home or their insurance company without first consulting an attorney. Facilities often move quickly after an incident to limit their liability, and documents presented as "routine" or "required" may actually waive your legal rights. Contact DeFatta Law Firm immediately before signing anything — our consultations are free and confidential.
How much does it cost to hire a nursing home abuse attorney? +
DeFatta Law Firm handles nursing home abuse cases on a contingency-fee basis — meaning you pay nothing upfront and owe no attorney fees unless we win your case. Your initial consultation is completely free and confidential. We believe every family deserves access to quality legal representation regardless of their financial situation.
